Why I Joined AltSchool Africa

I joined AltSchool to improve my technical skills in frontend development. As someone with a product background, I wanted to learn how to build interfaces myself, collaborate better with engineers, and gain the versatility to take on more hands-on product and engineering roles. AltSchool's affordability and focus on practical learning made it the ideal choice.

One of the most defining chapters of my career has been building and leading a startup called Bidhaa—a learning platform that connects aspiring African entrepreneurs with practical business and product knowledge. We’re building an ecosystem that equips students and young professionals with contextual tools to develop viable startup ideas. We’ve reached users across Nigeria and the UK, and the feedback has been encouraging. But leading a startup as a non-technical founder has also revealed the biggest gap in my toolkit: technical implementation.

That’s why I decided to enroll at AltSchool Africa, choosing the Front-End Engineering track. It’s a strategic decision—front-end skills will not only help me build better, more intuitive user experiences for our platform, but also allow me to collaborate more effectively with engineering teams. Learning HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and React in a structured, project-based environment will give me the hands-on experience I’ve been missing. I’m ready to get my hands dirty—not just managing products, but building them.
